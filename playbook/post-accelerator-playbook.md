# LinkedIn Post Accelerator Playbook

## Purpose

This is a practical, step-by-step playbook for maximising the reach of
important LinkedIn posts. It translates the algorithm research into
actions you can execute on every significant post.

Not every post needs the full playbook. Use the tiered approach below
to match effort to importance.

---

## The 360Brew Context

LinkedIn's algorithm was overhauled in late 2025 with a 150-billion-
parameter AI model called 360Brew. The key implication for this
playbook: LinkedIn now evaluates **you**, not just your posts. Your
profile, posting history, topic consistency and engagement patterns
are assessed holistically before your content enters the distribution
pipeline.

This means the playbook now has two layers: **content-level actions**
(what to do with each post) and **profile-level foundations** (what
to maintain over time). Both matter.

---

## When to Use This

**Full playbook (all steps):** Company announcements, major thought
leadership pieces, campaign launches, posts tied to breaking news,
anything promoting a lead-generation asset.

**Light version (steps 1, 4, 6 only):** Regular thought leadership
posts, industry commentary, engagement-building content.

**Skip entirely:** Quick replies, reshares, casual observations.

---

## Profile-Level Foundations (Ongoing)

These are not per-post actions but ongoing disciplines that determine
how 360Brew treats your content over time. Review quarterly.

### Maintain Topic Consistency

Post consistently about 2–3 related topics for 60–90+ days. 360Brew
uses this sustained focus to build your topic authority score, which
directly affects how widely your posts are distributed. Scatter your
posts across unrelated topics and the algorithm cannot determine who
should see your content.

### Align Your Profile to Your Content

Your headline, About section and professional experience define your
content niche in 360Brew's interpretation. If your profile says
"Managing Director, Security" but you post about generic productivity
tips, the algorithm sees a mismatch and suppresses distribution.
Domain-specific language that could only come from a practitioner
carries more weight than generic advice.

### Manage Your Engagement Footprint

Who you comment on, what you react to and where you spend time on the
platform tells 360Brew which professional community you belong to.
Engaging broadly with random content prevents the algorithm from
placing you in a relevant content community. Be deliberate: engage
primarily within your subject domain and with people in your target
audience.

### Posting Frequency

The optimal cadence is **3–4 posts per week**. Each new post
cannibalises the distribution lifecycle of the previous one — a high-
performing post now has an extended lifespan of 48–72 hours under
360Brew, so daily posting cuts that short. Focus on making each post
genuinely valuable rather than filling a content calendar for
consistency's sake.

## Pre-Publish (Day Before or Morning Of)

### Step 1: Run the Virality Checker

Score the post using the LinkedIn Virality Checker. Target 70+ before
publishing. Address any flagged issues, particularly:

- Weak hooks or withheld openings (manufactured suspense)
- AI tells: both lexical (word-level) and rhetorical (structural
  patterns like false binaries, stage directions, generic question
  closes)
- Service-pitch tone or fear-based messaging
- Template conformity (Hook → Body → CTA without variation)

The virality checker's rhetorical pattern scan is particularly
important under 360Brew. Posts that trigger multiple AI rhetorical
patterns face up to 47% less organic reach.

### Step 2: Check Content Is Save-Worthy

Under 360Brew, saves have become one of the most critical ranking
signals — a post with 200 saves dramatically outperforms one with
1,000 likes. Before publishing, ask: "Would someone bookmark this to
refer back to later?" Content that drives saves includes:

- Frameworks, checklists or decision trees
- Original data or benchmarks
- Step-by-step processes with specific detail
- Contrarian perspectives backed by evidence
- Anything a reader would want to share with their team

If the post is pure commentary with no reference value, consider
whether it can be strengthened with an actionable takeaway.

### Step 3: Brief Your Early Engagement Team

Identify 3–5 people who will engage in the first 30 minutes.
Prioritise:

- A business partner, co-founder or close colleague — this is the
  most important single commenter
- 2–3 contacts who are senior professionals in your target audience's
  domain — expert comments carry 5–7x more algorithmic weight than
  random connections
- Anyone with a large, relevant network who might reshare

Send them a brief message the evening before or morning of:

> "Publishing a post about [topic] at [time] tomorrow. Would
> appreciate a genuine comment in the first 30 mins if it resonates
> with you. No pressure to agree — a counterpoint or question is just
> as valuable."

Do not ask people to "like and comment" generically. LinkedIn detects
engagement pod patterns with 97% accuracy. The comments must be
genuine and substantive. 360Brew also analyses **lexical diversity**
in comment sections — if multiple comments share similar phrasing, the
algorithm treats them as coordinated and downgrades reach.

### Step 4: Check Timing and Spacing

- Publish **Tuesday to Thursday, 9–11 AM** in your target audience's
  timezone
- Confirm at least **12 hours** since your last post
- Confirm at least **48 hours** since a high-performing post (let it
  complete its distribution lifecycle under 360Brew before publishing
  again)
- Confirm you haven't published three text-only posts in a row (vary
  format to avoid the 20%+ reach penalty)
- If this is a newsjack tied to breaking news, prioritise timeliness
  over optimal day/time

## The Golden Window (First 30 Minutes)

This is the most important half hour. The algorithm decides in this
window whether to push the post to a wider audience. 75% of total
reach is determined here.

### Step 5: Be Present and Responsive

- **Clear your calendar** for 30 minutes after posting. Do not post
  and walk away. Under 360Brew, post-and-ghost behaviour (publishing
  then not engaging) is interpreted as low commitment and reduces
  distribution.
- **Reply to every comment** that arrives, ideally within 5–10 minutes
- **Make your replies substantive** (15+ words). Don't just say
  "Thanks!" or "Great point." Add a follow-up thought, ask a question
  back or build on what they said.
- **Try to spark threads between commenters.** If Person A and Person
  B both comment, reply to Person A referencing Person B's point:
  "Interesting — @PersonB raised a similar question about [X]. Curious
  whether you've seen the same pattern." Posts with 3+ participants
  exchanging views receive 5.2x amplification.

### Step 6: Colleague Cross-Engagement

If you have a business partner or close colleague, the one who didn't
write the post should:

- Leave a **genuine, substantive comment (15+ words)** within the
  first 15 minutes
- The comment should add a perspective, share a related experience or
  pose a thoughtful follow-up question
- **Do not** leave a "Great post!" comment. This signals
  inauthenticity and adds no algorithmic value. Under 360Brew, generic
  comments are classified as low-entropy noise and may actually harm
  the post's distribution.
- If others comment, engage with their comments too — this creates the
  threading multiplier

Good cross-engagement comment examples:

> "This connects to something I've been seeing in conversations with
> [sector] organisations — they know they have a [problem] but don't
> know how to surface it without creating a blame culture. The
> [approach] changes that dynamic completely."

> "Worth adding that the [trend] is accelerating faster than most
> people realise. We had three conversations last month where [topic]
> was a new section on the [process]. Twelve months ago it wasn't
> mentioned."

## Post-Publish (Hours 1–72)

Under 360Brew, a high-performing post has an extended distribution
lifecycle of 48–72 hours. This means the post-publish phase matters
more than it did previously.

### Step 7: Continue Engaging

- Check back at **lunchtime (12–2 PM)** and **early evening** for new
  comments
- Continue replying substantively to every comment
- If the post is performing well (high comment count, shares, saves),
  consider sharing it to a company page with added context
- **Day 2–3:** Continue monitoring. Posts that receive sustained
  engagement over 48–72 hours can achieve 340% longer active
  distribution time under 360Brew. A thoughtful reply to a late
  comment can re-trigger distribution.

### Step 8: Capture and Learn

- After 72 hours (extended from 48 under 360Brew), note the post's
  performance: impressions, engagement rate, comment count and — if
  visible — **saves**
- Compare against previous posts — what worked differently?
- Feed learnings back into future content planning
- If the post performed exceptionally well, consider repurposing the
  topic into a carousel, newsletter article or blog post (but wait at
  least 2 weeks — recycled content within that window receives 84%
  less reach)

## Quick Reference: Signal Value Hierarchy

When deciding where to spend your engagement time, focus on the
highest-value actions. Under 360Brew, saves and comment quality have
been elevated while likes have been devalued.

| Action | Algorithmic Value |
|--------|------------------|
| Sparking a 3+ person comment thread | 5.2x amplification |
| Comment from a senior industry figure | 5–7x a random connection's comment |
| Save | Very high — now one of the most critical signals |
| Long comment (15+ words) | 2.5x a like |
| Standard comment (5+ words) | 2x a like |
| Like | Baseline (lowest value, significantly devalued under 360Brew) |

One genuine comment thread between three industry-relevant people is
worth more than 30 likes.

## What Not to Do

- **Don't use engagement pods.** LinkedIn detects them with 97%
  accuracy. Accounts caught in pods experience shadowbans with reach
  dropping from thousands to hundreds overnight. 360Brew analyses
  comment velocity, account relationship patterns and engagement
  timing to identify coordinated activity.
- **Don't ask for generic engagement.** "Please like and share"
  triggers engagement bait detection. LinkedIn recognises 70+
  engagement bait patterns.
- **Don't post and disappear.** An unanswered comment in the first 30
  minutes is a wasted opportunity. Each reply you leave also counts as
  fresh engagement on the post. Under 360Brew, post-and-ghost
  behaviour actively reduces distribution.
- **Don't edit the post after publishing.** Edits reset the
  distribution clock and can reduce reach significantly.
- **Don't delete and repost.** LinkedIn's algorithm remembers deleted
  content and may penalise the repost.
- **Don't post daily.** Under 360Brew, each new post cannibalises the
  previous one's distribution lifecycle. 3–4 posts per week is the
  optimal cadence.
- **Don't scatter topics.** Posting about AI security on Monday,
  personal productivity on Wednesday and cooking on Friday prevents
  360Brew from building your topic authority. Stay within your 2–3
  defined content pillars.
